About This Project

In this section of the website, you can read and learn more about substance abuse, its effects, and statistics in college-aged students.

Spreading Awareness

This project puts forth into the spotlight a very prevalent and prominent issue in our modern collegiate world where alcohol and drugs have taken the wheel of many students that leads to their undoing. My purpose? Spreading awareness. Stating the facts. Opening the eyes to everyone, namely college students the dangers of substance abuse in a collegiate setting and what could happen to those who fall prey to addiction or even just a moderate consumption.

The Dangers of Substances

Obviously we are aware that there are negative effects when it comes to consuming alcohol or drugs. Below I have provided a list that I have researched from multiple research organizations (see the sources section) to show the impact that substances can take on a student's life:

  • Academics and grades will start to decline
  • Increased probability for car-related accidents
  • More prone to be a victim of an overdose
  • Unstable mental health state (depression, anxiety, withdrawal, personality disorders)
  • Higher chances for family crises and dysfunctions
  • Will endure more of a financial struggle
  • More likely to get into legal trouble

Substance Abuse Statistics

There are some scary statistics regarding substance abuse on a college campus and to help spread awareness and prevalence of this issue below will have some statistics on this issue.

  • Around 28% of students engaged in binge drinking in 2018
  • Over 14 million of the age of 12 or older has had Alcohol Use Disorder
  • Above 20% of students were exposed to cocaine in the year of 2021
  • In 2010, Alcohol misuse cost the United States $249 billion
  • Approximately 3,000 individuals under the age of 21 have died as a result of alcohol consumption
  • In 2016, nearly 40% of students have used marijuana that year
  • Only about 2.3% of college students receive mental health services or treatment in 2019-2020 (down from 4.9% in 2010-2011)
